# SMS vs email — when to use each

> Both channels live in the same Inbox, but they get very different reply rates depending on the situation. Pick the right one and your candidates will thank you.

## Before you start
- SMS requires a phone number on the candidate's profile and an SMS-enabled plan.
- Always start with email for the first contact unless the candidate opted in to SMS.

## When to use email

1. First-touch outreach, scheduling longer interviews, sending offers, and anything that needs attachments or formatted content.
   ![Email composition](./assets/01-email.png)
2. Compliance-sensitive exchanges where you need a clear written record and unsubscribe footer.

## When to use SMS

1. Quick confirmations — interview reminders, "running 5 mins late," asking for a missing document.
   ![SMS composition](./assets/02-sms.png)
2. Re-engaging a candidate who hasn't opened your last few emails. SMS reply rates are typically several times higher for short prompts.

## Tips
- Keep SMS under 160 characters when possible and always identify your company in the first line.
- Don't switch channels mid-conversation without asking — it confuses candidates.

## Troubleshooting
- **SMS option is hidden** — the candidate has no phone number, or your plan doesn't include SMS.
- **Candidate replied "STOP"** — they've opted out. Switch to email and don't message them on SMS again.
