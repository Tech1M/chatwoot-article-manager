# Read your job insights

> Find out which channels work, where candidates drop off, and how your job is performing.

## Before you start
- The job needs to be published — drafts don't have insights.
- Allow at least a day for meaningful numbers to show up.

## Steps

1. Open the job and click the **Insights** tab.
   ![Insights tab](./assets/01-insights-tab.png)
2. The top row shows key numbers — views, applications, qualified candidates, and time to hire.
   ![Headline metrics](./assets/02-metrics.png)
3. Scroll to the **Funnel** chart to see where candidates drop off between stages.
   ![Funnel chart](./assets/03-funnel.png)
4. Use the **Channels** breakdown to compare which sources are bringing in the most qualified applicants.
   ![Channels breakdown](./assets/04-channels.png)

## Tips
- A high view count with few applications usually means the description or screener questions are scaring candidates off.
- Pause underperforming premium channels and put the credits into ones with stronger conversion.

## Troubleshooting
- **Numbers look stuck at zero** — give it 24 hours after publishing. If still empty, confirm the job is actually published.
- **Channel data is missing** — some channels only report weekly. Check back later in the week.
