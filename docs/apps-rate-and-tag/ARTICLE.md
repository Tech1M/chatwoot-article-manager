# Rate and tag applications

> Use ratings to express how strong a candidate is and tags to group them by skill, source, or anything else your team tracks.

## Before you start
- Decide what your team's ratings mean (for example, 1 = no, 3 = maybe, 5 = strong yes). Consistency matters more than the exact scale.
- Agree on a small set of tags so they stay useful — for example, `senior`, `referred`, `relocation-ok`.

## Steps

1. Open a candidate from the job's **Applications** tab.
   ![Open candidate](./assets/01-open-candidate.png)
2. In the rating control on the candidate's profile, click the score you want to give. Your rating is saved immediately and shown next to your name.
   ![Rate candidate](./assets/02-rate-candidate.png)
3. To add a tag, click **Add tag**, type a label, and either pick an existing tag from the suggestions or press **Enter** to create a new one. Tags appear on the candidate card and in the list view.
   ![Add tag](./assets/03-add-tag.png)
4. Back in the applications list, filter by rating or tag to focus on a slice — for example, "everyone tagged `referred` with a rating of 4 or above".

## Tips
- Ratings are per teammate. The list can show your rating, the team average, or both, so debrief discussions stay grounded in everyone's view.
- Tags work best when a small group of people own them. Too many one-off tags make filtering noisy.

## Troubleshooting
- **You don't see a rating control** — your role may not include rating permission. Ask an admin to update your role.
- **A tag won't save** — check for a typo or trailing space; tags must be at least two characters.
