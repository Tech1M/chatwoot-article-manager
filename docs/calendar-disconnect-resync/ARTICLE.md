# Disconnect or resync your calendar

> Disconnect a calendar when you no longer want Tech1m to read or write to it, or trigger a resync when availability looks stale.

## Before you start
- You can only disconnect calendars that you connected. Other teammates' calendars are managed in their own settings.
- Disconnecting cancels future syncs — events Tech1m already added stay on your Google Calendar.

## Steps

1. Open **Settings → Calendars**. Each connected calendar is listed with its status.
   ![Connected calendars list](./assets/01-calendars-list.png)
2. To force a refresh, click **Resync** next to the calendar. Tech1m re-reads your busy times and upcoming events.
   ![Resync calendar action](./assets/02-resync.png)
3. To remove the connection entirely, click **Disconnect** and confirm. The calendar drops out of free-time checks and smart scheduling immediately.
   ![Disconnect confirmation](./assets/03-disconnect.png)

## Tips
- Resync after large changes in Google Calendar (bulk-deleted events, a new shared calendar) so Tech1m picks them up faster.
- If you switch Google accounts, disconnect the old one first, then connect the new one — this avoids two calendars competing as your default.

## Troubleshooting
- **Resync keeps failing** — disconnect and reconnect the calendar. The reconnect refreshes the access Tech1m needs.
- **Events from a deleted calendar still show** — give it a few minutes after disconnecting, then refresh the page.
- **You can't see a Disconnect button** — only the person who connected the calendar can remove it.
