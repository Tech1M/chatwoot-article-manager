# Pipeline analytics

> Spot stages where candidates pile up or drop out so you can fix the funnel, not just measure it.

## Before you start
- Define your pipeline stages on the job before reviewing analytics.
- Move some candidates through stages so the funnel has data.

## Steps

1. Open **Insights**, then click the **Pipeline** tab.
   ![Pipeline tab](./assets/01-pipeline-tab.png)
2. Pick the job (or all jobs) and date range you want to study at the top of the page.
3. Read the **conversion funnel** from left to right. Each stage shows how many candidates entered and the percentage that moved to the next stage.
   ![Conversion funnel](./assets/02-funnel.png)
4. Check the **time-in-stage** chart below to find where candidates wait the longest. Stages shaded red are above your target SLA.
5. Click a stage bar to see the exact candidates currently sitting there so you can nudge them forward.
   ![Stage drill-in](./assets/03-stage-drill.png)

## Tips
- Compare two date ranges to see whether a process change actually improved conversion.
- Group by recruiter to see if a stall is a stage problem or a workload problem.

## Troubleshooting
- **Funnel shows 0%** — candidates have not been moved between stages yet, or a stage was renamed.
- **Time-in-stage looks wrong** — check that stage moves are happening in Tech1m, not tracked elsewhere.
