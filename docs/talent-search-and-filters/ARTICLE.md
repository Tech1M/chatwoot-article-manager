# Search and filter candidates

> Narrow down a pool to exactly the people you want to message, shortlist, or export.

## Before you start
- Open a talent pool that has candidates in it.
- Know roughly what you are looking for — a job title, a skill, a city, or a tag.

## Steps

1. In a talent pool, type into the **Search** box at the top. Tech1m searches across name, headline, skills, current company, and notes.
   ![Search box](./assets/01-search-box.png)
2. Click **Filters** to add structured filters: location, skills, years of experience, tags, favorite status, source, and date added.
   ![Filters panel](./assets/02-filters-panel.png)
3. Combine filters by stacking them — each filter narrows the result further.
   ![Stacked filters](./assets/03-stacked-filters.png)
4. Once you have the right list, you can save the search, run a bulk action, or export the results.
   ![Save search](./assets/04-save-search.png)

## Tips
- Search supports natural phrases like "react developer in Nairobi" — try a sentence first before clicking into filters.
- Save searches you use often (for example, "Senior backend with Python") so you do not have to rebuild them.
- Toggle the **Favorites only** filter to focus on shortlisted people first.

## Troubleshooting
- **No results** — relax one filter at a time; combined filters can be too strict.
- **Older candidates not appearing** — check whether you are inside a single pool. Switch to "All candidates" to search across pools.
