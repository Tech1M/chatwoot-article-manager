# Handle sensitive answers

> Open-ended assessments occasionally surface answers that need careful handling — disclosures of harm, protected-class information, or content you don't want passed downstream.

## Before you start
- An assessment session containing the answer in question.
- Your organization's policy on flagged content (who to escalate to, retention rules).

## Steps

1. Open the candidate's session. On any answer, click the menu and choose **Flag as sensitive**. Pick a reason — wellbeing, protected attribute, off-topic, or other.
   ![Flag answer menu](./assets/01-flag-answer.png)
2. The flagged answer is hidden from default views and replaced with a placeholder. Reviewers without the **Sensitive content** permission won't see the original text.
   ![Redacted placeholder view](./assets/02-redacted-placeholder.png)
3. Add an internal note explaining the flag and, if needed, click **Escalate** to notify your designated safety or compliance contact.
   ![Escalate to contact](./assets/03-escalate.png)
4. When sharing results, flagged answers are excluded automatically — both from candidate-facing and external shares.

## Tips
- Flag rather than delete. The original answer is preserved for compliance review and can be unflagged by an admin.
- If a candidate self-discloses something concerning, follow your local duty-of-care guidance before responding.

## Troubleshooting
- **Can't see a flagged answer** — your role doesn't include the **Sensitive content** permission. Ask a workspace admin.
- **Flag won't apply to a code-challenge submission** — flagging is available on text answers and uploads. For code, leave a reviewer note instead.
