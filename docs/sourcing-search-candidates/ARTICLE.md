# Search for candidates with AI

> Find sourced candidates by combining a natural-language query with structured filters.

## Before you start
- You need permission to view AI Sourcing in your workspace.
- Have a clear idea of the role, key skills, and locations you want to target.

## Steps

1. Open **AI Sourcing** from the left navigation, then go to **Search candidates**.
   ![AI Sourcing search entry point](./assets/01-search-entry.png)
2. Type what you're looking for in plain English — for example, *"senior backend engineer with Go and Kubernetes in Berlin"*. Add filters for skills, country, current company, or job role to narrow the results.
   ![Search bar with filters open](./assets/02-search-filters.png)
3. Review the candidate cards. Each one shows the headline, current company, top skills, and which contact channels are available (LinkedIn, email, phone).
   ![Candidate result cards](./assets/03-results.png)
4. Click a candidate to open the full profile with experience, education, projects, and certifications. From there, add them to a talent pool or enroll them in a sequence.

## Tips
- Start broad, then tighten filters one at a time — you'll see how each filter shifts the result set.
- Use the **has email** or **has LinkedIn** filters when your outreach plan needs a specific channel.
- Recent searches appear in the side panel so you can jump back without retyping.

## Troubleshooting
- **No results** — loosen one filter at a time, or simplify the search text. Very specific queries can return zero matches.
- **A filter isn't supported** — you'll see a clear message naming the unsupported field. Remove it and try again.
- **Results look stale** — refresh the page; the index updates continuously.
