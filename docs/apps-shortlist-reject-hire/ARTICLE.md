# Shortlist, reject, or hire a candidate

> These are the three decisions that change a candidate's status for everyone — your team, your reports, and the candidate.

## Before you start
- Make sure the rest of the team has had a chance to weigh in on close calls. Check ratings and notes first.
- Have a rejection email template ready if your team uses one — you can pick it during the rejection step.

## Steps

1. **Shortlist** — open the candidate (or select several) and click **Shortlist**. They move to the **Shortlisted** stage. Use bulk shortlist on the list view to advance several promising candidates after a screening pass.
   ![Shortlist action](./assets/01-shortlist.png)
2. **Reject** — click **Reject** on the candidate. Choose a reason and decide whether to notify the candidate by email now, later, or not at all. The notification is optional but recommended.
   ![Reject action](./assets/02-reject.png)
3. **Hire** — once the candidate has accepted an offer, click **Hire**. This locks the application as hired, closes any remaining offer reminders, and updates your hiring reports.
   ![Hire action](./assets/03-hire.png)

## Tips
- You can reject a candidate from any stage — they don't need to be in **Applied**.
- Bulk-reject the rest of a job's pipeline once you've hired, so candidates aren't left waiting for a response.

## Troubleshooting
- **Hire is greyed out** — the candidate must be in **Offered** with an accepted offer first.
- **Rejection email didn't go out** — check the candidate's activity log; if you chose "don't notify", no email is sent. You can resend from the candidate's profile.
