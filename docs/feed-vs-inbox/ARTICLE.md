# Feed vs Inbox — what goes where

> Both surfaces are for messages, but they serve very different purposes. A few simple rules will keep your team's communication clean.

## The short version

1. **Feed** is for one-to-many internal updates — announcements, wins, policy changes, polls, kudos.
   ![Feed post example](./assets/01-feed.png)
2. **Inbox** is for one-to-one or small-group conversations, mostly with people outside your team — candidates, contractors, partners — about a specific job or contract.
   ![Inbox conversation example](./assets/02-inbox.png)

## When to use the Feed

- The whole company (or a department) benefits from seeing it.
- You want reactions, comments, or a confirmation count.
- The information should stay searchable for everyone, not just two people.

## When to use the Inbox

- The recipient is a candidate, contractor, or external partner.
- The thread is about a specific application, brief, or contract.
- The content is private to the people involved.

## Tips
- If a Feed post turns into a long back-and-forth with one person, move it to the Inbox or DM and link back.
- Use targeted Feed posts instead of mass-emailing departments — the engagement and tracking are far better.

## Troubleshooting
- **Posted to the wrong place** — delete and repost; both surfaces support quick deletion.
- **Not sure which to use** — ask: "Does anyone other than the recipient need to see this?" If yes, it's a Feed post.
