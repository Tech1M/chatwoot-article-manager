# My payment failed

> A charge or renewal didn't go through and you've received a payment failure notice.

## Symptom

You see a banner at the top of the workspace, or an email, telling you a payment failed. Some features may be limited until the charge succeeds.

## Most likely cause: the card on file was declined

The most common reasons are an expired card, insufficient funds, or your bank flagging the charge as unusual.

1. Go to **Settings → Billing**.
2. Open **Payment methods** and check the card on file.
   ![Billing payment methods](./assets/01-payment-methods.png)
3. If the card is expired or close to expiring, click **Add new card** and replace it. Set the new card as default.
4. Confirm the billing address matches what your bank has on record — a mismatch is a common silent decline.
5. Click **Retry payment** on the failed charge. You should see a success confirmation within a minute.

## Other things to check

- Your bank may have blocked the charge as suspicious. Approve it in your banking app, then retry.
- Daily card spending limits — try a different card or ask your bank to raise the limit.
- Currency or international fees blocked by the issuer.
- Your finance team uses a shared card that's been rotated. Update with the new details.
- A pending charge from an earlier attempt — wait an hour for it to clear before retrying.

## When to contact support

Reach out if the retry keeps failing with the same card, your bank confirms the charge wasn't blocked on their end, or you need an invoice reissued for a different payment method. Include the failed charge date and the last four digits of the card.
