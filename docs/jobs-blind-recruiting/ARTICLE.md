# Turn on blind recruiting

> Mask names, photos, and other identifying details so your team reviews candidates on skill alone.

## Before you start
- Make sure your team knows blind mode is on so they don't request the hidden details elsewhere.
- Plan when identifiers should be revealed — by default this happens at the interview stage.

## Steps

1. While creating or editing a job, open the **Blind recruiting** step.
   ![Blind recruiting step](./assets/01-blind-step.png)
2. Toggle **Enable blind recruiting** on.
   ![Toggle on](./assets/02-toggle.png)
3. Review the list of fields that will be hidden — candidate name, photo, gender, age, social links, and educational institution among others.
   ![Hidden fields list](./assets/03-hidden-fields.png)
4. Continue to the next step. Identifiers will reappear automatically once a candidate reaches the interview stage.

## Tips
- Pair blind recruiting with structured screener questions and scorecards for the strongest bias-reduction effect.
- Tell your interviewers that hidden fields will become visible once they advance someone.

## Troubleshooting
- **I can still see the candidate's name** — admins and the job owner see the full record. Reviewers in earlier stages see masked details.
- **Pictures are showing in emails** — only the in-app review surfaces are masked. Avoid forwarding raw applications outside the platform.
